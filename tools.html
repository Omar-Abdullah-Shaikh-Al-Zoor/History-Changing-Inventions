The Invention of the Compass: No More Blind Travel!

    When it comes to air and marine transport, it is rational to say that no one likes to blindly head into a storm and drown in the ocean or have their wings clipped, therefore we use a compass to guide us in our travels. According to Wolchover and Leggett, the Han dynasty period, more specifically the period between the 2nd century BC and the 1st century AD, marked the invention of the first compass. They have also mentioned that the compass was made using a naturally magnetized iron ore called “lodestone” and was first used for navigation between the 11th and 12th century (2021). Since then, up until today, and most definitely throughout the next few centuries, the compass continues to be a tool that supports trade and travels between faraway lands and nations.

The End of the Candle and Lantern Era: The Invention of the Incadescent Lightbulb

    Humanity's dependence on natural light is an era bygone. This was marked by the invention of the first lightbulb, which was collaborated on by many inventors. However, Palermo and McKelvie mention that the creation of a complete and functional lighting system that consists of a generator, wiring, and a bulb with carbon-filament had earned Thomas Edison the right to be called the main inventor (2021). According to the same authors, Edison and his team tested 3000 designs for lightbulbs during the period between 1878 and 1880. Not only that, they said that Edison has also spent an entire year after November 1979 trying to find the perfect plant to use as a carbonized filament for his lightbulb. According to Josephson during that time, Edison and his team found that carbonized bamboo filaments burn up to 1200 hours. This was later replaced with more efficient materials (1959). According to an article by Palermo and McKelvie, “William David Coolidge, an American physicist with General Electric, improved the company's method of manufacturing tungsten filaments in 1910” (2021). Ever since then, tungsten filaments have become a primary element used in incandescent light bulbs even today. The invention of the lightbulb has revolutionized how the world population lives. It has allowed us to be productive during the day and late in the night, not to mention the fact that cities look bright as day during the night.
